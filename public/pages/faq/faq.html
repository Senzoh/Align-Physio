REQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS - FAQ

Everything you need to know, answered.

Why should I visit a physiotherapist?

+

You should wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows for easy movement and access to the area being treated. If you're attending for a lower body issue, wearing shorts or loose-fitting pants that can be rolled up is ideal. For upper body concerns, a sleeveless top or a loose T-shirt works best. If your session involves movement assessments, athletic wear and trainers are recommended. Avoid jeans, tight clothing, or restrictive footwear, as they may limit movement and make assessment or treatment more difficult.

Is physiotherapy actually effective, or is it just temporary relief?

+

Physiotherapy isn’t just about temporary relief — it’s focused on correcting the root cause of pain and dysfunction. With consistent treatment and guidance, most clients experience long-term improvement, not just symptom masking.

What makes this clinic different from NHS or other private physios?

+

Unlike many clinics, we focus on rapid, personalised care with no long waiting lists. Each session is designed around you, not a generic protocol — and we offer ongoing support after your appointment too.

Do I need a referral from a GP?

+

No referral is needed to book with us. You can come directly to a physiotherapist for assessment and treatment — saving you time and letting you take control of your health straight away.

What if my pain doesn’t improve after a session?

+

Recovery takes time and consistency. While some feel better after one session, others need a few to see lasting change. We monitor your progress and adjust your plan as needed — you’re never left guessing.

Is this just for athletes or people with injuries?

+

Not at all. We help people from all walks of life — from desk workers with back pain to seniors working on balance. If you want to feel better, move better, or avoid future issues, we’re here for you.
