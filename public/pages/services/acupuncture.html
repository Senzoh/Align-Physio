What Is Medical Acupuncture?

Medical acupuncture involves the use of fine, sterile needles inserted into specific points in the body to stimulate natural pain-relieving chemicals like endorphins and serotonin, improve blood circulation, and relax tight muscles. Unlike traditional acupuncture, this method is grounded in modern science and tailored specifically to musculoskeletal conditions.

  • ✓Increases blood flow to support tissue repair
  • ✓Relaxes overactive muscles
  • ✓Regulates nerve sensitivity, especially in chronic pain
  • ✓Reduces inflammation and promotes recovery
  • ✓Complements other physiotherapy treatments
  • ✓Safe, effective, and non-invasive

REQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS - FAQ

Everything you need to know, answered.

What conditions can acupuncture treat?

+

Acupuncture is commonly used to treat pain (e.g. back, neck, arthritis), headaches, anxiety, insomnia, digestive issues, fertility challenges, and more. It helps rebalance the body’s natural energy (Qi) and promote healing.

Does acupuncture hurt?

+

Most people feel little to no pain during acupuncture. You may feel a mild tingling, dull ache, or warmth where the needles are inserted. The needles are very thin—much finer than those used for injections.

How many sessions will I need?

+

The number of sessions depends on your condition and goals. Acute issues may require only a few sessions, while chronic conditions often benefit from regular treatments over several weeks.

What should I do before and after treatment?

+

Before treatment, avoid heavy meals and caffeine. Wear loose, comfortable clothing. Afterward, stay hydrated, rest if needed, and avoid strenuous activity or alcohol. Let your body process the changes.
